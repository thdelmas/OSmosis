# Osmosis Manifesto

## 1. You bought it, you own it

If you paid for a device, you should be able to run whatever software you want on it. Locked bootloaders, vendor restrictions, and forced obsolescence are problems, not features. Ownership means control — not a permanent rental agreement disguised as a purchase.

## 2. No device left behind

A perfectly good device doesn't become e-waste just because the manufacturer stopped sending updates. Hardware outlives software cycles by years. Osmosis exists to keep devices alive, useful, and secure long after their makers have moved on.

## 3. Privacy is non-negotiable

You shouldn't have to trade your data for a working phone. We prioritize ROMs and firmware that respect your privacy and give you control over what phones home. Telemetry should be opt-in, not baked into the OS you never chose.

## 4. Complexity is our problem, not yours

Flashing firmware can brick devices. Partition tables, boot images, recovery modes, download protocols — none of this should require a weekend of forum research. Our job is to make the process as safe, guided, and reversible as possible, so the risk falls on our engineering, not on your courage.

## 5. Built for everyone — literally everyone

If a twelve-year-old can't figure it out, we failed. If a grandparent is scared to click the next button, we failed. If someone who has never opened a terminal feels lost, we failed. Osmosis must be so intuitive that technical background is irrelevant. Plain language over jargon. Clear steps over clever interfaces. Friendly guidance over assumptions of knowledge. The people who benefit most from device freedom — kids breathing life into a hand-me-down phone, older adults dodging planned obsolescence, anyone who simply shouldn't need a computer science degree to use what they own — are exactly the people we build for first. No one should feel like this isn't for them.

## 6. Every platform, every form factor

Phones today, routers and cars tomorrow. If it has a chip and a flash method, it belongs on our roadmap. We refuse to accept that freedom applies only to one class of device. A Raspberry Pi, a Samsung tablet, a boat chartplotter, and a smart light bulb all deserve the same respect.

## 7. Open source or go home

Osmosis is free software. The tools we wrap — Heimdall, fastboot, adb, esptool — are open source. We will never depend on proprietary blobs we can't audit. If we can't read the code, we can't trust the code, and neither should you.

## 8. Teach, don't hide

Every command we run is shown to you. Dry-run mode exists so you can learn before you commit. We'd rather you understand what's happening than blindly trust us. Osmosis is a tool, not a black box — and the best outcome is a user who no longer needs us.
